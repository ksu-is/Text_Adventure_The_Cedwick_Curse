You decide to search the rows of pews, you run your hand along the first wooden row. 

You lift your hand and find it completely blackened from the dust that had accumulated in who knows how long. 

You wipe it off on your shirt and continue to look. 

Scanning each pew and the holders on the back of the previous pew. 

You find nothing but dust and putrid smell.

As one of the pews you held onto for balance crumbles to the ground, sending you to the floor, covered in dust and a moldy stench.

“That’s enough of that.” You say.
