It was hallow’s eve, but you are not celebrating. In fact, no one is celebrating. 

There was a horrendous storm. 

The bright flash of the lightning and the loud booming of the thunder, echoed in your mind as you ducked under tree branch after tree branch, 
trying to find a suitable shelter to weather out the storm. 

The rain poured down and the wind gusted so fast that you could barely see two feet in front of you. 

The clothes are stuck to your skin like tar. 

Your bare feet stepped on fallen branches, its rough bark cracking under your foot. 

You feel a rumbling from your stomach, a sad reminder that you had nothing to eat for at least two days, maybe more. You can not remember.

You stumble upon a metal fence and use it to claw your way through the storm. 

Finally, you reach two stone pillars with an open metal gate and make your way up the driveway. 

Before you, you see a white figure and it's calling out to you, “Heeeeelp…meeee.  Heeeeelp meeee.…”
